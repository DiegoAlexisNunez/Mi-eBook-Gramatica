1. Verb to be

Explanation: The verb to be is used to indicate identity, state, or condition. It is conjugated as am, is, or are depending on the subject.

Examples:

  • I am a student.
  • She is at home.
  • They are my friends.

1. I a student.

2. She not at home.

3. they your friends?

2. Verb have

Explanation: The verb have is used to indicate possession or relationships. It is conjugated as have or has depending on the subject.

Examples:

  • I have a car.
  • He has two brothers.
  • Do you have a pen?

2. He two brothers.

3. Do you a pen?

3. Subject Pronouns

Explanation: Subject pronouns are used to replace the subject of a sentence. They include I, you, he, she, it, we, they.

Examples:

  • I am going to the store.
  • He is my friend.
  • They are playing soccer.

4. Article A and An

Explanation: Articles a and an are used to refer to indefinite nouns. A is used before words starting with consonant sounds, and an is used before words starting with vowel sounds.

Examples:

  • She has a book.
  • I ate an apple.

Choose the correct article (a or an) for the following sentences:

1. I have dog.

2. He saw elephant.

5. Demonstrative Pronouns

Explanation: Demonstrative pronouns point to specific things. They include this, that, these, those.

Examples:

  • This is my book.
  • Those are my friends.

6. There is / There are

Explanation: We use there is for singular nouns and there are for plural nouns.

Examples:

  • There is a book on the table.
  • There are three apples in the basket.

7. Word Questions

Explanation: Word questions are used to ask for specific information. They include what, where, when, why, how.

Examples:

  • What is your name?
  • Where do you live?

8. Object Pronouns

Explanation: Object pronouns replace the object of the sentence. These include me, you, him, her, it, us, them.

Examples:

  • Can you help me?
  • I saw him at the park.
  • They invited us to the party.

9. Possessive Pronouns

Explanation: Possessive pronouns show ownership. These include mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs.

Examples:

  • This book is mine.
  • That pen is hers.
  • The house is ours.

10. Possessive Adjectives

Explanation: Possessive adjectives show ownership but are used before a noun. These include my, your, his, her, its, our, their.

Examples:

  • This is my book.
  • That is their house.
